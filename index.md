Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 14, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains what data QR & Barcode Scanner-HyperFast ("the App," "we," "us") collects, how it is used, and the choices and rights available to you. It applies to the App only (package name com.hyperfast.qrscanner), available on Google Play.

If you have questions this policy doesn't answer, contact us using the details in Section 9.

1. Summary

Read the rest of this policy for full detail, but in short:


The App's core scanning and generation features work entirely on your device and do not require an account or sign-in.
We do not operate any server that receives your scans, generated codes, or personal information.
The App shows ads using Google AdMob, which may collect certain device and advertising data as described in Section 3 — this data collection is performed by Google, not by us directly.
The only data we store ourselves is scan/generation history and your settings, saved locally on your device, under your control.


2. Data the App collects directly

QR/barcode scanning and generation happen entirely on your device using Google's ML Kit on-device library and the ZXing library. Scanned content and codes you generate are never uploaded to us — we don't operate any servers that receive this data.

Your scan history, favorites, and settings are stored only in a local database on your device. This data:


Is never transmitted off your device by us.
Is deleted automatically if you uninstall the App.
Can be cleared at any time from within the App, or by clearing the App's storage/data from your device's system settings.


The App requests the following device permissions:


Camera — required to scan QR codes and barcodes. Camera access is used live, in-app, and no photo or video is stored or transmitted by us unless you explicitly choose to save a generated QR code image.
Internet / network state — required by the Google AdMob advertising SDK described in Section 3, to request and display ads.
Vibrate — used only if you enable vibrate-on-scan in settings.


We do not collect your name, contacts, precise location, or any account or identity information ourselves, and the App does not require you to sign in or create an account.

3. Advertising (Google AdMob)

The App shows ads using Google AdMob. AdMob may collect device identifiers (such as the advertising ID), approximate location, and general usage data to serve and measure ads, and may use this data consistent with Google's own policies. This data collection is performed by Google, not by us directly — we do not receive or store this advertising data ourselves.

You can review and control this via:


Google's Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy
Google's Ads Settings: https://adssettings.google.com
Your device's ad personalization settings (Settings → Privacy → Ads on most Android devices)


Consent. Where required by applicable law (including the EU/EEA, UK, and certain US states), the App presents a consent form before requesting personalized ads, using Google's User Messaging Platform (UMP). No ad is requested until your consent status has been determined. You can review or change your choice at any time via the device ad-personalization settings linked above.

If you would prefer an ad-free experience with no advertising SDK at all, a separate paid version of this App, QR & Barcode Scanner-HyperFast Pro, is available on Google Play with its own, separate privacy policy.

4. Data you choose to share

If you generate a QR code containing personal information (for example, your phone number, email address, or a Wi-Fi password) and then share or save that QR code image, that information becomes embedded in the image file itself. Once shared or saved, that image is only as private as you make it — the App does not add any additional tracking, watermarking, or identifying data to it beyond what is described in Section 3, and has no ability to retract or control the image after it leaves the App (e.g. after you share it via another app).

Similarly, if you scan a QR code created by someone else, we do not see, log, or have any way to access the content you scan ourselves. Whatever data is embedded in that code is decoded locally on your device.

5. Legal basis for processing (EEA/UK users)

For the data we collect and process directly (Section 2), we are not, in the ordinary sense, a "controller" or "processor" of your personal data under the GDPR or UK GDPR, because that data is not transmitted to us and functions entirely on-device, for the sole purpose of providing the App's core scanning/generation functionality you have requested.

For advertising data collected via Google AdMob (Section 3), Google acts as an independent controller for its own ad-serving purposes, and the App relies on your consent (via the UMP consent flow, where required) as the legal basis for enabling personalized advertising.

6. Your rights

For data stored locally on your device (scan history, favorites, settings), you have direct and complete control at all times:


Access — view your scan history and favorites at any time within the App.
Deletion — delete individual history/favorite entries in-app, clear all App data via your device's system settings, or uninstall the App to remove everything.
Portability — since we hold no copy of this data ourselves, the data on your device is the complete and only copy we are aware of.


For advertising data collected by Google AdMob, your rights and choices are governed by Google's own privacy policy and ad settings, linked in Section 3.

If you are located in the EEA, UK, California, or another jurisdiction with statutory privacy rights and believe this does not fully address a right available to you, contact us via Section 9 and we will respond.

7. Children's privacy

The App is not directed at children under 13 (or the relevant age of digital consent in your country), and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children ourselves. If you believe a child has provided personal information through the App, contact us at the address in Section 9 and we will address it.

Continued use of the App after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the revised policy. If a change is significant, we will make reasonable efforts to highlight it (for example, in the App's release notes).
